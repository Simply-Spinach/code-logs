Code Log 1: HTML Semantic Tags

Semantic Tags are tags in html that describe what the object is beyond just a functional perspective. For example, take a header, defined with <h1&gh. There is very little defined as to what a "header" exactly is. Is it the title of a book, is it the beginning of a chapter or is it the name of a website? Without a human reading what&aposs inside the header, it&aposs very hard to tell what exactly this header is. While that may not matter as much to the average browsing experience, there are certainly users who do care about these intricacies. Accessibility tools like text to speech will interact with these elements better as long as they are defined, for example, and even non-human "users", website scrapers, are important to accommodate as Google&aposs scrapers will use semantic elements to determine what's important for them from your website and what is not important to them.

Some examples of semantic tags are:

  • <article>: A section that can be taken "out of context" of it's orgin and still understood.
  • <header>: Where the primary header is of a document.
  • <title>: Title of a document
  • <nav>: Navigation elements.
  • <aside>: Content in a side bar
  • <section>: A generalized grouping element
And many more...
